Privacy Policy for Auto Relay

Last updated: April 12, 2026

Overview

Auto Relay is a message forwarding app. It reads incoming SMS messages on your device and forwards them to a destination you configure — either your Gmail account or another phone number. The app does not operate any servers and does not transmit your data to the developer.

Data Collected

DataPurposeRetention
SMS message contentForwarding to your configured destinationIn-app log, 14 days
SMS sender (phone number or name)Included in forwarded messageIn-app log, 14 days
Google account (email address)Identify forwarding destinationStored locally on device

No data is collected by the developer. All data stays on your device or is sent directly to your own Gmail account.

Google API and Gmail

Auto Relay uses the Gmail API solely to send forwarded messages to your own Gmail account. The app's use of Gmail API data:

  • Is limited to sending email on your behalf to your own account
  • Is not used for advertising or any purpose unrelated to forwarding
  • Is not transferred to third parties
  • Complies with the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements

Permissions

  • RECEIVE_SMS / READ_SMS — read incoming SMS messages for forwarding
  • SEND_SMS — forward messages to another phone number (if configured)
  • INTERNET — send emails via Gmail API
  • Notification access — detect incoming RCS messages via Google Messages notifications

Data Sharing

Your message data is forwarded only to the destination you configure. It is never sent to the developer or any third party.

Data Deletion

You can clear the in-app activity log at any time via the overflow menu. Revoking SMS permissions or notification access stops the app from processing new messages. You can revoke Gmail access at any time via your Google account settings at myaccount.google.com.

Contact

For questions about this privacy policy, contact: auto-relay-testing@googlegroups.com
